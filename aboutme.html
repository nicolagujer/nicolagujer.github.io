About Me

Who on Earth am I?

I am currently in my final semester of my Bachelor of Science (majoring in Physics and Mathematics) at the University of Auckland. One of my main interests has always been in space and I think that is how I found myself studying physics at university. This is also why my honours project for my dissertation is in observational astronomy. That, and I simply couldn't turn down an opportunity to use such a fun-looking telescope. I hope to keep my blog updated with the progress in this project.

When I am not busy writing down many numbers and formulas then crossing them out and starting again, I spend time making data visualisations using D3. I stumbled across this hobby through a Research Assistant role I gained over the summer of 2015/2016 working with the University of Auckland and Te Punaha Matatini. What started as a "Hey wouldn't it be neat if you made this into an interactive visualisation..." comment then turned into the rather time consuming project that is now my NZ Academics on Twitter visualisation, and it turns out that I really enjoyed making it. So from that project I have taken the opportunity to continue making the odd visualisation and trying to develop my skills further.

If there is any time left in my day I will most likely be found in my kitchen baking or if I am not there I am probably hiding with a book somewhere.
